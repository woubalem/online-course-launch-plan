Days 1–30
Phase One

Foundation + Build

Build the product, messaging, and infrastructure — validate before you record.

The #1 mistake coaches make: Recording 40 hours of content before confirming anyone wants to buy it. Spend your first 7 days validating demand through direct conversations, then build with confidence.

01
Validate First Critical
  • Send 10+ DMs: "Would you pay $X for Y?"
  • Open a beta waitlist before recording anything
  • Define your transformation: clear before → after
  • Write your ideal customer profile (age, situation, pain)
  • Confirm your core promise and measurable outcomes
  • Pre-sell to 3–5 people at a founding member discount
02
Product Development
  • Map full course outline: modules, lessons, deliverables
  • Record 30–50% of content (enough to sell confidently)
  • Upload initial modules to Thinkific
  • Build student-facing workbooks or templates for each module
  • Create completion checklists per lesson
  • Plan 2–3 live Q&A calls to include as a bonus
03
Offer & Pricing Strategy
  • Choose price point ($297–$997 depending on depth)
  • Build bonus stack: templates, live calls, community
  • Add urgency lever: cohort start date or limited-time bonus
  • Design checkout page with bump offer + guarantee
  • Place testimonials and key results above the fold
  • Write a strong, specific guarantee (30 or 60 day)
04
ActiveCampaign Setup
  • Create lists + tags: Lead, Registered, Attended, No-show, Purchased
  • Build webinar registration confirmation automation
  • Build pre-webinar reminder email sequence (3 emails)
  • Build post-webinar sales sequence (7–10 emails)
  • Set up post-purchase welcome + onboarding automation
  • Connect Thinkific → AC for purchase tagging
05
WebinarJam Setup
  • Create webinar event with compelling outcome-driven title
  • Configure registration page or integrate with landing page
  • Set up backup reminder emails inside WebinarJam
  • Prepare on-screen offer slide and chat poll triggers
  • Set up replay page for no-show follow-up
  • Configure just-in-time webinar dates for evergreen later
06
SMS + Thinkific Onboarding
  • Integrate Twilio with ActiveCampaign via Zapier or native
  • Build webinar reminder texts: 24hr · 1hr · 10min before
  • Build cart-closing SMS reminder for launch week
  • Write 5-minute post-purchase welcome SMS (reduces refunds)
  • Build Thinkific onboarding sequence: welcome video + quick win lesson
  • Set up progress milestone emails inside Thinkific
Webinar Script Structure
Recommended timing for a 60-minute conversion webinar
0–5 min
Hook + Pattern Interrupt
Open with a bold statement or counterintuitive truth. Preview the transformation they'll get today. "By the end of this, you'll know exactly how to [result] — even if [common objection]."
5–12 min
Credibility + Your Story
Why you, why now. Share your journey without over-explaining. Make it about them: "I struggled with this too, then I discovered…" End with a student success story or result.
12–40 min
3 Core Teaching Points
Teach genuine value — real insights, not fluff. Each point should stand alone as useful, but leave them wanting the full system. Structure: Problem → Insight → Example → Mini-result.
40–50 min
Transition to Offer (Practice This Most)
Natural bridge: "What I just taught you is a fraction of what's inside [course name]." Reveal the offer, price, and bonus stack. Anchor the value: "This alone would cost you $X if you hired someone."
50–60 min
Close + Live Q&A
Restate the offer, remind of the deadline/bonus. Address top 3 objections before they're asked (time, money, "is this for me?"). End Q&A by circling back to the CTA one final time.

Days 31–60
Phase Two

Audience Build + Pre-Launch

Drive leads and warm your audience toward the webinar with consistent content and precision email nurture.

01
Lead Generation
  • Post content 5–6x/week using your content system (not 6 raw pieces)
  • Primary CTA: register for free webinar
  • Optional: lead magnet (free guide or checklist) as top-of-funnel entry
  • Daily DMs to warm audience members
  • Partner with 1–2 adjacent creators for cross-promotion
  • Repurpose every piece of content to maximize reach per effort
02
Webinar Promotion
  • Promote for 2–3 full weeks before the webinar date
  • Mix: organic social + direct outreach + email pushes
  • Problem-focused content in the final week (not just "join my webinar")
  • Run a 3-day countdown on stories / short-form
  • Email your list 3× during final week (don't be shy)
  • Target: 100–300 registrants for your first webinar
03
Finalize the Course
  • Complete 80–100% of all modules
  • Upload and organize all content in Thinkific
  • QA user experience: login, navigation, video playback
  • Record a warm welcome video to greet new students
  • Build the onboarding sequence: quick win in lesson 1 within 24 hrs
  • Set up student progress milestone emails
Pre-Webinar Email Sequence
ActiveCampaign · 5–7 emails over 7–10 days
  • Day 1
    Welcome + What's coming
    Confirm registration, set expectations, tease what they'll learn. Keep it short and warm.
  • Day 2
    Problem agitation (send this before your story)
    Name the exact pain they're in. Make them feel understood. Don't pitch yet — just connect deeply.
  • Day 4
    My story + the shift that changed everything
    Share your personal journey from struggle to solution. End with: "I'll show you exactly how on the webinar."
  • Day 5
    The one thing holding most people back (+ a quick win)
    Deliver real value — a tip, framework, or insight. Build trust and authority before asking them to show up.
  • Day 6
    Here's what [Student Name] achieved in 30 days
    Social proof email. Real result or case study. Let them see themselves in the transformation.
  • Day 7
    Reminder: We're live [day] at [time] — here's what to expect
    Final invitation push. Preview the exact 3 things they'll walk away with. Add urgency: limited live spots.
Webinar SMS Reminder Flow
Twilio via ActiveCampaign · Keep it human
  • 24 hrs out
    Hey [Name] — we're live tomorrow at [time]. Can't wait to show you [specific result]. Here's the link: [URL]
  • 1 hr out
    Starting in 1 hour! Grab a coffee and get ready — this is the one you've been waiting for. [URL]
  • 10 min out
    We go live in 10 minutes 👀 Clicking the link now will get you in before we start. [URL]
  • No-show
    Hey [Name], you missed a good one! The replay is up for the next 48 hours: [URL] — don't let it expire.
Keep SMS under 160 characters where possible. First name personalization doubles response rates. Always include the direct link — never make them hunt for it.
Days 61–90
Phase Three

Launch + Optimize + Scale

Convert your audience, analyze what's working, and build the evergreen system that sells while you sleep.

01
Host Live Webinars
  • Run 1–3 live sessions across the launch window
  • Record every session for replay funnel
  • Track show-up rate, watch time, and conversion % live
  • Engage the chat — call out names, run polls
  • Leave Q&A open for 10 extra minutes after the offer
  • Follow up with every attendee who didn't buy within 24 hrs
02
Social Media During Launch
  • Post daily: testimonials, behind-the-scenes, objection handling
  • Countdown posts create urgency — use stories and pinned posts
  • Share real-time wins from new students immediately
  • Address top 3 objections as individual posts (time, money, doubt)
  • Go live on IG/TikTok 1–2x during the launch window
  • Screenshot and share DMs + replies from engaged followers
03
Conversion Optimization
  • Review landing page conversion rate (target: 20–40%)
  • Review show-up rate (target: 25–35%)
  • Review sales conversion rate (target: 3–10%)
  • Test one variable at a time: headline, price, or bonus stack
  • Add affiliate or referral program for post-launch scaling
  • Survey non-buyers: "What almost stopped you from joining?"
Post-Webinar Sales Sequence
ActiveCampaign · 7–10 days · Cart closes on Day 7 or 10
  • Day 1
    The replay is live (expires in 48 hours)
    Send to all attendees and no-shows. Include the direct replay link prominently. Remind them of the offer.
  • Day 2
    The 3 things you need to remember from yesterday
    Key takeaway recap — re-teach the 3 core points briefly. Make non-attendees feel they missed something real.
  • Day 3
    FAQ: Every question from the webinar, answered (move this up — highest converter)
    Address the 5–7 most common questions. This email converts because it meets buyers at peak objection. Don't bury it at day 5.
  • Day 4
    Still on the fence? Read this.
    Objection handling: address time, money, and self-doubt specifically. Use "I hear you, and here's what I'd say..."
  • Day 5
    [Student Name] went from [before] to [after] in [timeframe]
    Your strongest testimonial or case study. Let the result speak. Include a photo if possible.
  • Day 6
    Doors close in 24 hours — here's what you get
    Full offer recap: course, bonuses, guarantee, price. Remind them why the price is a fraction of the value.
  • Day 7
    Final call: this is the last email
    Short, direct, urgent. "Doors close at midnight. Either you're in or you're not — I'll see you on the inside." Link only.

What Most People Miss

The Strategic Principles

01
Sell before it's perfect
A perfect course that nobody buys is worth nothing. Validate demand in the first 7 days with real conversations. Pre-sell to your first 3–5 students before you've finished recording. Imperfect and sold beats polished and invisible every time.
02
The webinar is your conversion engine
Every other element of this funnel exists to get people into the room and then follow up afterward. Don't rush the webinar script. Practice your offer transition until it feels completely natural. This is the highest-leverage asset you'll build.
03
Email does most of the selling
Most people buy on email day 3–7, not on the webinar itself. Spend disproportionate time on your post-webinar sequence. The FAQ email alone consistently outperforms everything else — put it on day 3 when objections are hottest.
04
Consistency beats virality
One viral video doesn't build a business. 30 days of consistent, problem-focused content builds an audience that trusts you enough to buy. Show up every day for 90 days and you'll have something that compounds. Wait for the perfect post and you'll have nothing.
05
Onboarding determines testimonials
The experience after purchase determines whether students get results, whether they refund, and whether they tell others. A quick win in the first 24 hours is worth more than a perfect module 6. Happy students are your best marketing for the next cohort.
06
Iteration is where revenue lives
Your first launch will be imperfect. Your second launch will be better. Your third will be strong. The coaches who succeed treat each launch as data, not a verdict. One variable at a time: first fix the offer, then the traffic, then the follow-up.
Reference

Terms & Definitions

A plain-English guide to the terminology used throughout this plan. Bookmark this page — you'll reference it often.

Call to Action
CTA

Any instruction that tells your audience what to do next. It could be a button, a line of text, or a verbal prompt during a webinar. Examples: "Click here to register," "Grab your spot," or "DM me the word READY." Every piece of content you create should have exactly one clear CTA — multiple CTAs create confusion and reduce action.

In this plan: During pre-launch, your CTA always points to the webinar registration page. During launch week, it points to the checkout page.
Just-In-Time Webinar
JIT Webinar

A pre-recorded webinar that appears to start "just in time" — usually within 15–30 minutes of someone registering, at any hour of the day. The viewer experiences it as a live event even though it was recorded in advance. This is what makes your funnel run 24/7 without you being present.

In this plan: After your live launches, you convert the recording into a JIT webinar inside WebinarJam so leads can convert at any time without you on camera.
Evergreen Automation
Always-on funnel

A marketing system that runs continuously in the background without requiring your active involvement. Once built, it automatically sends emails, triggers SMS reminders, hosts webinars, and processes sales — every day, regardless of whether you're working. "Evergreen" means it doesn't expire like a live launch does.

In this plan: This is the end goal of Days 61–90. You go from manual live launches to an automated machine that generates revenue passively.
Show-Up Rate
Attendance metric

The percentage of people who registered for your webinar who actually attend it live. If 200 people register and 60 show up, your show-up rate is 30%. This number is critical because people who attend live convert at a dramatically higher rate than those who only watch the replay. Industry average is 25–35% for warm audiences.

In this plan: Your SMS reminder sequence — 24hr, 1hr, and 10-minute texts — exists specifically to raise this number. Every percentage point matters.
Sales Conversion Rate
CVR · Close rate

The percentage of webinar attendees who purchase your course. If 60 people watch the webinar and 4 buy, your conversion rate is 6.7%. A healthy webinar conversion rate is 3–10% depending on price point, audience warmth, and how strong your offer is. This is the single most important number in your entire business.

In this plan: If your rate is below 3%, focus on strengthening the offer-to-transition moment in your webinar script and your post-webinar email sequence.
Cart Close
Deadline · Doors closing

The moment when enrollment officially ends and the course is no longer available to purchase (or the bonus stack expires). Cart close is not just a tactic — it's a genuine commitment to your audience that creates real urgency. Without a deadline, most people who are "thinking about it" never buy. A firm cart close is one of the most powerful conversion levers in a launch.

In this plan: Your cart typically closes 7–10 days after the webinar. Your final day email and SMS are the highest-converting messages in the entire sequence.
Quality Assurance
QA · Testing

The process of reviewing and testing every part of your system before it goes live to real customers. This includes clicking through your course as a student would, testing that emails send correctly, confirming checkout processes a payment, and verifying that SMS messages arrive at the right time. Skipping QA is the most common cause of embarrassing technical failures during a live launch.

In this plan: Run a full QA pass at the end of Days 31–60, before your first live webinar. Create a test account in Thinkific and go through the entire student experience yourself.
Live Q&A Bonus Call
Office hours · Hot seat call

A scheduled video call (typically on Zoom) where enrolled students can ask you questions directly and get personalized guidance. Including 2–3 live Q&A calls as a bonus dramatically increases the perceived value of your course because it signals ongoing access to you — something students pay a premium for. It also gives you direct insight into where students are getting stuck, which improves the course over time.

In this plan: Include 2–3 live Q&A calls in your bonus stack during the offer phase. Schedule them 2, 4, and 6 weeks after cohort start so they're spread across the learning journey.
Order Bump
Checkout upsell · Add-on offer

A small, complementary product offered directly on the checkout page — typically a checkbox the buyer ticks before completing their purchase. Because the customer already has their credit card out and is in a buying mindset, order bumps convert at an unusually high rate (20–40%). The bump should be low-ticket ($47–$97), quick to consume, and clearly enhance the main course purchase.

In this plan: Add an order bump to your Thinkific checkout page. Good examples: a done-for-you template pack, a one-page quick-start guide, or a recorded workshop that complements module 1.
Lead Magnet
Freebie · Opt-in offer · Content upgrade

A free resource you offer in exchange for someone's email address. It could be a checklist, PDF guide, template, short video training, or quiz. Its only job is to attract the right people into your email list by solving one specific, immediate problem they have — the same problem your course goes deeper on. A good lead magnet pre-qualifies your audience: people who want the freebie are likely to want the course.

In this plan: An optional top-of-funnel entry point during pre-launch. If you use one, make sure it naturally leads to your webinar registration as the next step.
Automation Trigger
Workflow trigger · Automation entry point

The specific action or event that starts an automated sequence inside ActiveCampaign. Common triggers include: someone submits a form, a tag is applied to a contact, a purchase is made, or a link is clicked. Think of a trigger as the "if this, then that" rule — if someone registers for the webinar, then automatically send the confirmation email and start the reminder sequence.

In this plan: Your key triggers are: webinar registration (starts pre-webinar sequence), webinar attendance (starts sales sequence), and Thinkific purchase (starts onboarding sequence).
Segmentation
List segmentation · Tagging

The practice of dividing your email list into distinct groups based on behavior, interests, or status — so you can send the right message to the right person at the right time. In ActiveCampaign, this is done through tags and lists. Someone tagged "Attended Webinar" should receive different follow-up emails than someone tagged "No-Show," even though both registered.

In this plan: Your core segments are Lead, Registered, Attended, No-Show, and Purchased. Never email all of these groups the same message — it hurts deliverability and conversions.
Bonus Stack
Value stack · Offer stack

The collection of additional resources, bonuses, and extras bundled with your core course to increase the overall perceived value of the offer. A strong bonus stack directly addresses the objections buyers have — if they worry "I don't have time," a bonus on time management tackles that fear head-on. Each bonus should have a clear, specific value that you state explicitly ("Bonus #1: normally $197, yours free today").

In this plan: Build your bonus stack before the webinar. Ideal bonuses: live Q&A calls, done-for-you templates, a private community, or a complementary mini-course.
Open Rate
Email open rate

The percentage of people on your email list who open a given email. If you send to 500 people and 175 open it, your open rate is 35%. Open rate is primarily influenced by your subject line, the sender name, and your list's overall trust in you. It tells you whether people are interested enough to look — but it doesn't tell you whether they took action. A healthy open rate for a warm course audience is 25–45%.

In this plan: If open rates drop below 20%, test new subject lines, clean inactive subscribers off your list, and check that your emails aren't landing in spam folders.
Nurture Sequence
Drip sequence · Email sequence

A series of pre-written emails sent automatically over time to guide a new subscriber from stranger to buyer. Each email serves a specific purpose — building trust, demonstrating expertise, handling objections, or making an offer. "Nurture" means you're warming the relationship over days or weeks before asking for a sale, rather than pitching immediately. This is fundamentally different from a one-off broadcast email.

In this plan: You have two main sequences: a pre-webinar nurture (5–7 emails warming leads toward attendance) and a post-webinar sales sequence (7–10 emails converting attendees into buyers).
Objection Handling
Reframe · Addressing resistance

The practice of proactively addressing the doubts, fears, and hesitations that prevent a potential buyer from purchasing — before they even voice them. The three most universal objections in online course sales are: "I don't have time," "I can't afford it," and "I'm not sure this will work for me." Addressing these directly and honestly in your webinar, emails, and social content removes the friction standing between a prospect and a purchase.

In this plan: Dedicate one post-webinar email entirely to objection handling (Day 4). Address each objection with empathy first, then logic, then social proof.
Social Proof
Testimonials · Case studies · Reviews

Evidence from real people that your course delivers on its promise. It includes written testimonials, video reviews, screenshots of results, before-and-after stories, and student case studies. Social proof is one of the most powerful conversion tools available because people trust other people's experiences more than they trust marketing copy. Even one strong, specific testimonial can meaningfully lift your conversion rate.

In this plan: Request testimonials from beta students or pre-sell buyers at the end of Days 1–30. Use them on your sales page, in your webinar, and in your post-webinar email sequence.
Landing Page
Opt-in page · Registration page · Squeeze page

A single, focused web page with one specific goal and one CTA — no navigation menu, no links out, no distractions. For a webinar funnel, the landing page exists solely to collect registrations. Everything on the page — the headline, bullet points, and button — should answer the visitor's question: "What will I get, and is it worth giving you my email?" A well-optimized landing page converts 20–40% of visitors into registrants.

In this plan: Your landing page drives traffic to your WebinarJam registration. Keep it focused: one headline, three to five benefit bullets, and a single registration button.
Cohort
Cohort-based launch · Live round

A group of students who start a course together on the same date and progress through the material as a community. Cohort-based courses typically have scheduled live calls, shared milestones, and a defined start and end date. The time-limited nature of a cohort creates natural urgency for buyers ("the next round starts March 1") and tends to produce better student results because of the shared accountability.

In this plan: Using a cohort start date as your urgency lever ("enroll by Friday to join the April cohort") is one of the most authentic and effective ways to close your cart without manufactured pressure.
Replay Funnel
Webinar replay · On-demand recording

The system for sending no-show registrants and post-webinar visitors to a recording of the live webinar, along with a follow-up email sequence to convert them. Replay viewers convert at roughly half the rate of live attendees — which is still significant revenue. The replay is typically available for 24–72 hours to maintain urgency. After your live launch, the replay becomes the foundation of your evergreen JIT funnel.

In this plan: Send the replay link on Day 1 of your post-webinar sequence to both live attendees and no-shows. Set a clear expiry time and communicate it in the email subject line.
